On-site project length: 6 months
Problem

The Toronto office of XL Catlin relied on a legacy, custom-built system for generating reports and client documents for corporate insurance customers. Over time, the underlying relational database—built on Claris FileMaker—had become complex, slow, and difficult to maintain, negatively impacting performance, data security, usability, and future scalability.

Industry

Insurance

These factors resulted in growing technical debt and increasing dissatisfaction among end users.

Business analyst role

My focus was on understanding the system from a business perspective by working closely with end users to identify pain points and define requirements for the refactored solution. I translated business needs into a clearer and more sustainable data model. In addition, I actively contributed to the technical refactoring of the system, including database relationship design, data mapping, view creation, and UI improvements, ensuring that the new structure better reflected real business processes.

Delivered outcomes

The refactored solution delivered a more performant, secure, and maintainable database structure, with improved usability for business users. My involvement significantly improved communication between the delivery side and system users, enabled a clearer understanding of business needs, and ensured those needs were more accurately reflected in the system's functionality, laying a solid foundation for future development and system growth.
